What is Air Pollution?

Air pollution is contamination of the indoor or outdoor environment by any chemical, physical or biological agent that modifies the natural characteristics of the atmosphere.

Common Sources of Air Pollution

Household Combustion

Indoor burning of fuels for heating and cooking

Motor Vehicles

Emissions from cars, trucks, and other vehicles

Industrial Facilities

Manufacturing and industrial processes

Forest Fires

Natural and human-caused forest fires

Major Public Health Concerns

Particulate Matter

Fine particles that can penetrate deep into lungs

Carbon Monoxide

Colorless, odorless gas from incomplete combustion

Ozone

Ground-level ozone forming from industrial emissions

Nitrogen Dioxide

Toxic gas from vehicle exhaust and power plants

Sulfur Dioxide

Industrial gas with severe respiratory effects

WHO Global Statistics

WHO data show that almost all of the global population (99%) breathe air that exceeds WHO guideline limits and contains high levels of pollutants, with low- and middle-income countries suffering from the highest exposures.
